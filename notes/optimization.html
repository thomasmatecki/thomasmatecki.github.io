Linear Programming

Ex. 1: Given \(f(x) = x^TCx\) and \(g(x) = b^Tx\), find critical points of \(f\) subject to \(g(x) = r\)

1) Set \( \nabla f(x) = \lambda \nabla g(x) \) and solve for \(x\) in terms of \(\lambda\). $$ x^T(C^T + C) = \lambda g^T $$ $$ x^T = \lambda g^T(C^T + C)^{-1}$$ $$ x = \lambda ((C^T + C)^{-1})^Tg = \lambda ((C^T + C)^T)^{-1}g = \mathbf{\lambda (C + C^T)^{-1}g}$$ 2) Substitute \(x\) in terms of \(\lambda\) into \(g(x) = r\) and solve for \(\lambda\). $$b^T \lambda (C + C^T)^{-1}g = r$$ $$\lambda = (b^T)^{-1}r((C + C^T)^{-1}g)^{-1} = \mathbf{(b^T)^{-1}rg^{-1}(C + C^T)}$$ 3) Substute \(\lambda\) in terms of \(r\) to determine \(x\) in terms of \(r\) $$x = (b^T)^{-1}rg^{-1}(C + C^T)(C + C^T)^{-1}g = (b^T)^{-1}r$$
