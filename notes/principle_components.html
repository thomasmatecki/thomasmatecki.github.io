Principle Components

Given an \(N \times p\) data matrix \(X\), the objective of principle components analysis is to transform the rows of \(X\) to a new coordinate system in which the columns are uncorrelated.

\( X = \begin{bmatrix} x_{11} & x_{12} & \dots & x_{1p} \\ x_{21} & x_{22} & \dots & x_{2p} \\ \vdots & & \ddots& \vdots\\ x_{N1} & x_{N2} & \dots & x_{Np} \end{bmatrix} \)

The object is to find a transformation from \(X\) to a matrix \(C\), which is made up of the principle components of \(X\).

The principle components are the column vectors \(C_i = Xe_i\) that satisfy \( e_i = \underset{e_i}{\mathrm{arg\min}} \left\{ \mathrm{var(C_i)} \right\} \)
